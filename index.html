My research focuses on numerical analysis and scientific computing. I develop highly efficient, high-fidelity numerical schemes for transport simulations, with applications in plasma physics, numerical weather prediction, and environmental fluid mechanics. Specific methodologies include high-order semi-Lagrangian methods, discontinuous Galerkin methods, and WENO schemes for hyperbolic conservation laws and kinetic equations.

Research Interests

Scientific Computing
∂

High-Order Numerical Methods

Finite difference and finite volume weighted essentially non-oscillatory (WENO) methods, and discontinuous Galerkin (DG) methods for hyperbolic conservation laws.

𝑓

Semi-Lagrangian Methods

High-order semi-Lagrangian schemes for kinetic and transport equations with applications in plasma physics, including Vlasov-Poisson and Vlasov-Maxwell systems.

⚡

Structure-Preserving Algorithms

Energy-conserving, asymptotic-preserving, and positivity-preserving schemes for multi-scale and multi-physics simulations on both structured and unstructured meshes.
